## About Me

<div style="text-align: justify">
I am a 3rd year PhD Student in Computational Statistics and Machine Learning at Imperial College London, supervised by <a href="http://wwwf.imperial.ac.uk/~nkantas/">Dr Nikolas Kantas</a>. I am jointly funded by EPSRC <a href="http://mpecdt.org/">Centre for Doctoral Training in the Mathematics of Planet Earth</a> and the <a href="https://www.npl.co.uk/">National Physical Laboratory</a>. I hold an MRes in Mathematics and an MSc in Statistics from Imperial College London, and an MA in Mathematics from the University of Cambridge.
</div>

## Research Interests

<div style="text-align: justify">
My research interests include the development and analysis of efficient computational methods for inference, learning, and optimisation in complex stochastic systems, and their applications to signal processing and machine learning. My current research focuses on the analysis of (two-timescale) stochastic gradient descent methods in continuous time, joint parameter estimation and optimal sensor placement in state space models, and online parameter estimation in high dimensional interacting particle systems.  
</div>
